welcome to the universal localization program

Contact: Rick Elliott
(relliott@netscape.com)
Last Updated: 05-Oct-98

The universal localization program (ULP) is intended to assist software developers, professional translators and other interested parties to create localized versions of Mozilla, as well as Netscape Navigator and Netscape Communicator Standard Edition client software. These localized products may be freely distributed under the translating party's own brand, according to the terms of the Universal Localizaton Program License Agreement.

Under the terms of this agreement, you may freely localize and distribute any version of Mozilla, or any version Netscape Navigator and Netscape Communicator Standard Edition products. You may localize into any language (some languages are better supported than others, however). You may localize for any platform, except Caldera Linux. The only hard requirements are that you

  1. Replace any and all Netscape trademarks with your own branding,

  2. Identify yourself as the translating party on the About: page,

  3. Assume all responsibility and liability for your work,

  4. Make your translated product freely available on the Internet.

The main form of assistance provided by the ULP comes in the form of localization kits. The localization kits include documentation, translation instructions, English language resources, and tools.

Participants in the ULP are encouraged to join the relevant Mozilla discussion groups. These are listed in the Community page. There are also other projects under way, related to internationalization and other translation or localization issues. You are encouraged to get involved in one of these or even start your own project.
