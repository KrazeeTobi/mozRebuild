community

We will soon be hosting mailing lists and newsgroups at mozilla.org, to foster open communication in the developer community. Our plans on exactly what to offer aren't concrete yet, since that depends on you, the developers. What forums do you want? As new special-interest groups appear, we will create new newsgroups or mailing lists, or whatever seems most useful. Let us know.

To start with, we have two newsgroups:

    news://secnews.netscape.com/netscape.public.mozilla.general
    This newsgroup is for discussion about any and all aspects of the Mozilla source code, and the things that we're doing here at mozilla.org. This is likely to be a high-volume group.

    news://secnews.netscape.com/netscape.public.mozilla.announce
    When new source releases become available, they will be announced here. This is a low-volume group, for announcements only (not discussions.)
Some people prefer mailing lists to newsgroups. Consequently, the newsgroups are also mirrored with a pair of mailing lists. This means that messages sent to one of the newsgroups will show up in its corresponding mailing list, and vice versa. That way, you get your pick of whether you would rather read the messages in news or in mail.

    mozilla-general@mozilla.org
    This mailing list mirrors the mozilla.general newsgroup. To subscribe, send mail to mozilla-general-request@mozilla.org with subscribe in the subject. To unsubscribe, send mail with unsubscribe in the subject.

    DO NOT send ``subscribe'' messages to ``mozilla-general''!

    Those messages will go to the entire list, and lots and lots of people will think bad things about you. It's a good way to lose friends and embarrass yourself in front of your peers. Always use the -request address for ``subscribe'' and ``unsubscribe'' messages. Just click on one of the mailto: links above, and everything will be fine.

    mozilla-announce@mozilla.org
    This mailing list mirrors the mozilla.announce newsgroup. To subscribe, send mail to mozilla-announce-request@mozilla.org with subscribe in the subject. To unsubscribe, send mail with unsubscribe in the subject.

In addition to the mailing lists and newsgroups, we may also provide other resources to foster communication among the developers. As always, we want to be flexible: let us know what would help you most!
