What is the purpose of this document?

The Bugzilla problem report database supports many possible resolutions which are selectable for open bugs.  This document outlines the suggested steps to take when verifying a bug set to a particular resolution.  It is critical for all involved in net community QA to be familiar with these guidelines in order to provide standardized synergy on the movement of bugs in support of each others various open source projects.

For a detailed guideline on verifying a bug set to a specific resolution, see the type of Resolved bug:
Fixed, Invalid, Won't Fix, Later, Duplicate, WorksForMe

Verifying Duplicate Bugs*
The most important thing to do when marking a Resolved/Duplicate bug as Verified/Duplicate is to ensure that the bug truly is a duplicate of another bug.
  • Read through duplicate to understand the context of the bug.
  • Click on the *** This bug has been marked as a duplicate of XXXXX *** link.
  • Read through the original bug to understand its context and ensure that it is indeed the same as the bug marked as its duplicate.
  • If the two bugs are indeed the same, you can change the Resolved/Duplicate to Verified/Duplicate.  Please always comment within the bug as you move it to Verified/Duplicate and that you have checked the original and comfirmed the duplicate.
*Note:  The following component QA Contact personnel and test engineers prefer to verify their own duplicates.  Please do not assist with of the following bugs.
  • Components: Internationalizaion, Localization
  • QA Contacts: elig@netscape.com
