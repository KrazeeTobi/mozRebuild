happy audit trails
 April 20th
Submitted by Paul Phillips <paulp@go2net.com> to Miscellaneous.

What have I been browsing lately?

I dunno. I've got an offensively large, probably corrupt global history of every URL I've ever seen, and I've got a session history that persists for as long as the browser doesn't crash that tracks my progress in a straight line like a cop looking for DUIs. Walk the line, heel to toe, forward, back, forward back. I browse sideways, where's my button?

It would be super cool if I could find out what I've really been doing. There are a million possible approaches to this, but a good start would be storage of my last N invocations of the browser and full information on the path traversals I took. Then display this in some treelike format that doesn't chop off leaves whenever I take a fork in the road.

Combined with some crypto goodness and an external authority, maybe this could be used in some interesting authentication ways. "Where were you on the night of the 27th?" "I was reading suck.com! Look at my authenticated audit trail!" "Okay, this guy's clean." Maybe that's a little far fetched, but remember, we're thinking outside the box here.
